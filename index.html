about

I am a philosopher working on how cultural values and social norms shape the behavior of artificial intelligence systems, with a focus on conversational agents and large language models. I hold a PhD in Philosophy from Sorbonne Université - CNRS, where my research examined the ethics of conversational AI at the intersection of moral philosophy and machine learning.

I currently work in the science team at Mistral AI, focusing on model behavior, with particular attention to cultural and multilingual alignment. Prior to this, I spent nearly four years at Hugging Face as Principal Ethicist, conducting interdisciplinary research on AI ethics in open source settings. Before moving into AI research, I worked on human rights policy at the European Parliament.

research

My work draws on centuries of moral philosophy to explore how AI systems are designed, aligned, and evaluated as they move beyond being simple tools and begin to take on social and relational roles, including emerging forms of AI companionship. I have been working on conversational AI since 2017, treating it as a sociotechnical system. In this context, I am especially interested in how AI shapes social relations at different levels, from individual interactions to broader collective and population dynamics. More broadly, I work on questions of human-AI interaction, AI evaluation, governance, safety, and AI ethics, always trying to connect philosophical reflection with how AI systems are actually developed and deployed.

media

My findings have been featured in international media including Nature, The New York Times, The Washington Post, MIT Technology Review, Wired, Bloomberg, Business Insider, and TechCrunch, as well as European outlets such as Le Figaro, La Repubblica, and Wired Italia.

I have written three op-eds in English for Tech Policy Press: on why debates about AI consciousness distract from more urgent issues, on Popper's paradox of tolerance and content moderation, and on what AI can learn from social media's mistakes before exploiting our chats. More recently, I wrote an op-ed for Wired Italia on how systems like ChatGPT are becoming the new mirrors of loneliness.

I regularly take part in public debate through television, podcasts, and radio, with appearances on BFMTV, TF1, RAI, and France24, as well as audio discussions for France Culture and L'ADN.
